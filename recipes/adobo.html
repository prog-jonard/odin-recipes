Adobo Recipe

Description

Filipino adobo is a beloved national dish where meat (typically chicken, pork, or a combination) is braised in a savory, tangy sauce made of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and whole black peppercorns. It is usually simmered until tender, leaving a rich, flavorful glaze that is classically served over steamed white rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb meat (chicken, pork, or beef)
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Marinate the meat in the vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat the cooking oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  3. Remove the meat from the marinade and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Reduce heat to low and cover the pan.
  5. Simmer for 20-30 minutes or until the meat is tender.
  6. Serve over steamed rice.
